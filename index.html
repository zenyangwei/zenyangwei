Nigel Gresley

The Fast History of
LNER Dynamometer Car

Built in 1906,

by the North Eastern Railway (NER) at Darlington Works, the car is a mobile laboratory used principally to measure the speed and power of a locomotive.

Most steam locomotives in the UK did not have speedometers in the cab until the 1950's, and even after that some were not fitted with them. The mobile laboratory also enabled it to measure factors which may affect a steam locomative's ability to pull:

Steam flow from the boiler to the cylinders


Steam temperature in the cylinders


Coal consumption


Water usage


It was also used to determine the exact locations of Stations/Signal boxes and tabulates how the locomotive was worked by the driver in detail.

Modern Reflection:

Imagine how the Dynamometer car would look today. Here's how the curators view it:

Most of the Dynamometer's features can be easily and accurately replicated with modern digital technologies. Modern trains have built in speedometer and fuel flow meters to accurately measure fuel consumption. If more data is needed, they can implant sensors onto the train such as GPS and connect it to a portable computing device to monitor changes.

However, we cannot fault the mechanical ingenuity needed to perform this task in an era where high-tech digital devices, which makes this a marvel piece of engineering.
